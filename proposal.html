PerfectSip By Sofia Walton

Project Concept/Motivation

“I want to build a coaster/cup accessory or attachment that mesures the temperature of the cup/mug and once it senses the “perfect sipping temperature” it lights up an RGB LED to notify the user that their drink is ready to sip on.”

Anticipated Bill of Materials

  • RGB LED strip (for notifying when the temp is ready for sipping!)
  • Thermistor (for temp sensing)
  • Resistor (to create voltage divider with thermistor)
  • Transistor (for swtiching the LED strip)
  • 12V power supply (for powering LEDs)
  • 9V battery (for powering arduino)
  • 3D printed coaster/cup attachment

Timeline & Contingency Plans

  • 1: Research temperature ranges for ideal beverage sipping and prototype a thermistor circuit on my breadboard.
  • 2: Integrate LEDs and calibrate temperature thresholds.
  • 3: Pototype a cardboard version of the coaster while thinking about: the thermisistor placement & hiding other components (breadboard/arduino)
  • 4: Final iterations on coaster design & CAD integration. Begin 3D printing coaster.
  • 5: Assemble the final prototype and make adjustments based on testing.
  • Contingency Plans: If I run into issues with the thermistor accuracy, I will explore using a different type of temperature sensor such as a digital temperature sensor. If power supply for the LED strip becomes an issue, I will consider using a lower power LED solution or reducing the number of LEDs used.
